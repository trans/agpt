# Commercial Licensing

This repository is released under the
[PolyForm Noncommercial License 1.0.0](LICENSE) for academic
research, personal study, and use by educational, research, or
non-profit institutions. **Commercial use requires a separate
license.**

## Who needs a commercial license

You (or your organization) likely need a commercial license if you:

- Integrate this code — or derivatives of it — into a commercial
  product, service, or internal business process at a for-profit
  organization.
- Host it as a paid service, or as a service that supports a
  commercial offering.
- Use the trained-model artifacts or the training pipeline to
  produce models that you distribute or monetize commercially.
- Are unsure which side of the line you fall on — the noncommercial
  terms are conservative, and the simplest answer is to ask.

If you are an individual experimenting, a student, a researcher at
an educational or research institution, or a non-profit contributor,
the noncommercial license already covers you — no separate license
needed.

## What a commercial license covers

- Right to use, modify, integrate, and distribute the code for
  commercial purposes, with terms appropriate to the scale and
  nature of use.
- Appropriate patent-license terms for commercial deployment.
- Optional: negotiated support, integration assistance, custom
  development, or collaboration on scale experiments.

Specific terms (flat fee, subscription, royalty, per-deployment,
hybrid, or collaboration-based) are sized to the deployment and
discussed directly. Startup-friendly and academic-spinout terms
are available.

Email **`transfire@gmail.com`** with:

- Your organization (size, stage, industry if relevant).

## FAQ

**Can we evaluate the code before committing to a license?**
Yes. The noncommercial license permits internal research,
experimentation, and evaluation. Once you move toward production use
for commercial purposes, we'll need to have the licensing
conversation.

**Do you sell a perpetual license or subscription?**
Both are possible; terms depend on deployment scale. Say what fits
your business; if the model doesn't fit I'll say so clearly rather
than wasting your time.

**Is the codebase actively maintained?**
The project is active research. A commercial license can include
agreed-upon maintenance and update terms, or explicitly exclude them
at a lower price. This is one of the things to discuss.

**We want to hire the author rather than license.**
Also a conversation worth having. Email with specifics.
