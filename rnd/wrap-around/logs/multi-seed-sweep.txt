Baseline (n=4):  mean=7.0375, range=6.93–7.13 (spread 0.20)
Space-cut (n=3): mean=7.1648, range=7.00–7.37 (spread 0.36)

space-cut is 0.13 PPL WORSE on average than baseline. Note that space-cut's
train loss is consistently lower (~1.91 vs 2.00) — the cleaner synth is
easier to fit, but overfits and generalizes worse to real text.

Mechanism (most likely):
  1. space-cut discards the post-last-space tail of each leaf (~5 chars
     per wrap = ~1.7M chars across 10M synth tokens), losing real
     corpus content.
  2. Always-space-then-non-space-after-wrap teaches a synth-specific
     transition pattern that doesn't match real text.
  3. Wrap-glue noise was already absorbed by the model — gluings looked
     ugly to humans but didn't hurt real-corpus prediction.
